Be Your Own Bartender!

We're here to bring basic mixology to the masses. Many classic drinks are simple combinations of easy to acquire ingredients. Our logic engine lets you confidently select new options among tried and true classics.

Get your drink on! (Responsibly of course)

Get mixing!

We start with a base spirit (vodka, gin, rum, bourbon) then we go from there. We connect you to videos for all our recipes, no matter how simple. Mix with confidence.

Recipes

The heart of our project. Tasty classics with ingredients you should be able to acquire easily. Many of the best drinks only require 2-5 ingredients. We'll tell you what you need and how to use it.

Your Home Bar

Setting up a home bar? We got you. We'll help you figure out what you need to entertain, be able to make several classic drinks at home, and not break the bank.

Bars

Want to get fancy? Thinking of elaborate specialty drinks? Leave that to the professionals. We'll help you find the best cocktail bars near you. Don't spend your hard earned cash on specialty ingredients that you'll buy once, not get right, and then will sit in your cupboard and mock you.

Recipes and Videos through our network

We're partnering with major brands to learn how their products might be best enjoyed.

Why videos?

Because we want you to mix with confidence. Because there are no stupid questions. Because someone, somewhere, is trying to make a rum & coke in a wineglass and that's just wrong.

Why would I need a recipe for a Gin & Tonic? It's in the name.

Because ratios matter. Because there are no stupid questions. Because having the ingredients in the name doesn't tell you what glass to put it in, how much ice, or the best garnish (lemon or lime, for the record).

How many options are there?

Depends. We help you select your options based on what's easy to find. Got vodka? Nice start. Mixes well. Want it with orange juice (Screwdriver)? Tonic (Vodka Tonic)? Orange juice and Peach Schnapps (Fuzzy Navel)? Orange Juice, Peach Schnapps, and Cranberry Juice (Sex On the Beach)? Vodka Martini? Vodka Sour? Add soda to that and have a Vodka Collins, take out the sour and it's a Vodka & Soda. We tell you what you can make with what you can get.
